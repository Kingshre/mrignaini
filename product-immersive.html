✦ The Heritage ✦

Born from centuries of craft

Every piece carries the soul of Jaipur — a city where artisans have been hand-carving wooden blocks and pressing them into fabric for over 500 years. This garment is not just clothing; it is a living tradition, passed down through generations of master craftspeople.

The prints you see tell stories of Mughal gardens, Rajasthani landscapes, and the wild flowers that bloom across the Aravalli hills. Each motif is hand-carved into teak wood blocks, then carefully stamped onto fabric — one impression at a time.

✦ The Making ✦

From block to beauty

01
🪵

Carving

Master artisans hand-carve teak wood blocks with traditional motifs — a process that takes days to perfect a single block.

02
🫧

Dahi Wash

The fabric is soaked in a mixture of curd, oil, and castor — an ancient Jaipur technique that softens the cotton and helps it absorb natural dyes.

03
🖌️

Block Printing

The carved block is dipped in natural dye and pressed onto the fabric by hand — each impression aligned with the precision of centuries of practice.

04
☀️

Sun Drying

Printed fabrics are laid out under the Rajasthani sun — the heat sets the natural dyes and deepens the colors to their final richness.

"Each mark is a memory — of the artisan's hands, the Jaipur wind, the morning light."
